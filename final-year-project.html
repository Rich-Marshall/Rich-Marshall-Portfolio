Vibes - Final Year PRoject

Aims

Analysis of my final year project for university. I developed a system that would allow teens an young adults to have a place to share events and follow theor favourite artists and promoters. I focused a lot on UX during this project both during development and research. This project shed a lot of light on the field of UX for me and ignited my passion for the field of UX for me.

Objective

With the sharp decline of Facebook use among the younger generations (looking specifically at late teens, 14-19), There emerges a gap in the market when it comes to events organising platforms. This project sought to create a platform that merges events organisation and social media that would be preferable to users in this age range.

The Tools

I began by conducting research around the problem and producing a literature review on “The Importance of UX and HCI on the Success of Systems for Adolescents”. Abstract: The purpose of this report is to examine the perhaps exaggerated impact that User Experience (UX) and Human Computer Interaction (HCI), as well as design, has on systems that are aimed towards a younger demographic (specifically 14-19year olds). This group is seen throughout the industry as one that values design and fluidity of systems almost as much as the functionality of systems themselves. Over recent years for example, there has been a rise in the use of Instagram and Snapchat by this age group and a decline in the use of Facebook. This report seeks to critically analyse why this group is so quick to move to and from platforms and how to set up and design a system that will be able to withstand the years and keep this group engaged for the foreseeable future.

I then began with requirements spec and UML including: Use Cases and descriptions, Class Diagrams, Entity Relationship Diagrams, and Data Flow Diagrams.


As a clearer picture of what was needed began to form, I started making pen and paper story boards that I could modify as I found better ideas for making navigation seamless and easy. Once happy with those I moved to a working document with more detailed designs on Adobe XD with a focus on keeping the screens sparse and easy to navigate with a modern design and typeface that would appeal to younger generations.

Live Site Landing Page

The Solution

By creating a clean, visually pleasing app that allows teens to not only plan events, but also interact almost directly with their favourite producers and promoters, vibes is able to fill the gap left by the Facebook events service in a way that is unlikely to be flooded by older generations. The key when designing for teens and young people is to ensure they have a safe space away from “adults”. Creating an app solely for this purpose kills two birds with one stone as it provides no real reason for the older generations to sign up.
